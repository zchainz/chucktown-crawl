Charleston Moisture Control

Complete Crawlspace Moisture Control for Charleston Homes

The Lowcountry is hard on crawlspaces. Between the humidity, the high water table, and the age of much of our housing stock, moisture finds its way in. Here's what that looks like — and how I fix it.

Why it happens here

Charleston sits in a humid subtropical climate with a high water table and marsh all around us. Many homes — especially older raised houses in places like West Ashley, James Island, and downtown — were built before modern moisture control was standard. Warm, wet air moves through an open crawlspace all year, and bare soil underneath wicks groundwater straight up into the space.

Left alone, that damp air rises into the living space, drives up your power bill as your HVAC fights the humidity, and quietly works on the wood your house is sitting on.

What it causes — and the fix

  • Musty smells and higher humidity upstairs
  • Mold, wood rot, and soft subflooring
  • Flagged moisture on a home-inspection report
  • Power bills higher than they should be

The fix is a layered approach: a sealed ground vapor barrier first, then wall liner and pier wraps where the space needs full encapsulation, interior drainage for active water, and a dehumidifier through my licensed HVAC partners when the space needs to be fully conditioned. I'll recommend what your house actually needs — not the most expensive package.

How much does a crawlspace vapor barrier cost in Charleston?
I install 10 mil vapor barrier starting at $.90 per square foot, 14 mil reinforced barrier for $1.30 per square foot, and 20 mil for $1.70 per square foot, with all seams overlapped and taped. A typical 1,500 sq ft Charleston crawlspace runs $1,350 to $2,550 depending on material. My minimum job is $1,500.
What's the difference between a vapor barrier and full encapsulation?
A vapor barrier covers the ground to stop moisture rising from the soil. Full encapsulation adds sealed wall liner, wrapped piers, and usually a dehumidifier to completely condition the space. Many Charleston homes only need a quality ground barrier — I'll tell you honestly which one your house actually needs during a free inspection.
Do you handle repairs from home inspection reports?
Yes. I work with Charleston real estate agents and homeowners on inspection repair items, pre-listing moisture work, and buyer repair requests, with photo and video documentation for the transaction file.
Why is crawlspace moisture such a problem in Charleston?
The Lowcountry combines high year-round humidity, a high water table, and a large stock of older homes built before modern moisture control. Damp crawlspaces lead to musty smells, mold, wood rot, higher power bills, and problems at resale.
